Wayward Hero

Controls and Commands

Commands are phrased as basic imperitive sentances, such as go north. There are a number of verbs that are recognized, namely:
  • go, move, walk, travel for movement.
  • look, examine, perceive, view, find, search for looking around and searching.
  • take, pickup, pick, grab, gather for collecting items
  • draw, hold for selecting the wielded item
  • attack, hit for attacking enemies
  • use, eat, burn, ignite, light for using and consuming items

The Questions:

  • Originality: Q: What is original about your game?
    A: That is an interesting question. This game is a challenge, to me, the maker, to try and make somthing fun and interesting using a severely limited interaction system, trying to emulate the old days before graphics were a common part of computers. So, mechanics-wise, there is nothing fantastically original. However, for this class and for most games made nowadays, this is a completely original game, its rare to see a game with absolutely no graphics today. In fact, I can't even think of an example. The closest thing I have to graphics is colored text!
  • Technical Merit: Q: What is interesting about your game technology?
    A:

    This game has been a technical challenge from beginning to end. As of me writing this, it is matching my previous game at over 1100 lines of code. Without Phaser or any other engine to back me up, this project has truely been written from scratch. All the interaction is using the manipulation of the contents of a div in the HTML of the page, inserting paragraphs and spans.

    And that does not even bring me to the more difficult and interesting parts. I had to write a massive ammount of support code, from functions to remove an index in an array without leaving a hole, to random choice utilities.

    On top of that, I had to design a system of representing the game world. When working with phaser, it is pretty easy to just make a sprite for the player, and even include the player's hp as a field in that object. here I had to make every type from scratch. This includes Items, Inventories, Creatures, and WorldChunks. Those four make up the majority of interactions in the world.

    But how to have the player interact? I had to design a way to parse a sentance looking for specific verbs, even potentally large numbers of them, and take the appropreate action. This turned out to be a distinct challenge.

  • Prototype Postmortem: Q: What did you learn from this prototype? What was the easiest or hardest part of making it?
    A:

    As far as learning, I decided that there are many unique difficulties to building a game like this, namely, parsing natural language. It was far more challenging than I expected.

    The hardest part has been designing the nesecary data structures and classes, and writing all their support functions, that get used by the actual actions that players take. Over 500 lines of code (almost half in the project) is dedicated to these data structures, while around 300 lines define the actual actions the player can take. (which too were a massive challenge)
