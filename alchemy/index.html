Alchemy

Game Documentation (For a Friend!)
  • Originality: Q: What is original about your game?
                        A: The game is based off of the idea that adding ingredients to a mixture changes the property. Its often seen as a crafting system in games, but here, I am trying to make a game out of this idea.
  • Technical Merit: Q: What is interesting about your game technology?
                        A: The button interaction was a bit interesting to get done, particularly with so little code. I am really satisfied how it worked. Also, the progressbars were fun. I thought about using a Phaser extendion, but then decided to use the geometry objects to create them myself.
  • Prototype Postmortem: Q: What did you learn from this prototype? What was the easiest or hardest part of making it?
                        A: The hardest part was getting all the parts to play nicely. Particularly, learing the Phaser API. the HSVtoRGB function was really annoying to figgure out.

  • High Concept: Add ingredients to a mixture to get its properties right.
  • Theme: Chemist (Alchemy is the precursor to modern Chemistry)
  • Mandated Variety:
    Input:Indirect
    Randomness:Lots
    Play Style: Achiver, Craftsman
    Genre: Puzzle
  • Prototype Goal: Physics attraction of image to mouse.
  • Player Experience Goals: This game is designed to be a challenge, and easier to complete with multiple people. That is why the info on what ingredients do what is only avalable on a different webpage.
  • Gameplay: Mostly clicking buttons on the part of the player, and reading and talking on the part of the assistant.
  • Strategies: Luck and Hope! and having a good assistant.
  • Story/Setting/Premise: You have to make the properties of your alchemical mixture match the desired properties, without overflowing the cauldron, so you can pass your Alchemy Exam!
  • Target Audience: Bored folks on the internet.
  • Play Time: A few minutes for one game. A long time if you want to win!
